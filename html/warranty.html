Frequently Asked Questions

What does an extended warranty cover?

Our extended warranty plans cover mechanical and electrical failures that occur after your factory warranty expires. Depending on the plan you choose, coverage can include the engine, transmission, drive axle, electrical components, air conditioning, heating, steering, suspension, fuel system, cooling system, and more. Each plan has specific coverages and exclusions detailed in the warranty agreement.

When should I purchase an extended warranty?

The best time to purchase an extended warranty is while your vehicle is still under its original factory warranty. This ensures continuous coverage and typically results in lower premiums. However, we do offer plans for vehicles that are no longer covered by their original warranty, subject to an inspection and eligibility requirements.

Can I cancel my extended warranty?

Yes, our extended warranty plans come with a 30-day money-back guarantee if no claims have been filed. After this period, you can still cancel for a prorated refund based on time and mileage. A small administration fee may apply for cancellations after the initial 30-day period.

How do I file a claim?

Filing a claim is simple. If your vehicle experiences a covered mechanical failure, take it to any licensed repair facility. Provide the repair facility with your warranty information and ask them to contact our claims department for authorization before any repairs are performed. Our claims specialists will work directly with the repair facility to authorize and pay for covered repairs.

Are there any deductibles?

Yes, our plans typically have a deductible that applies to each covered repair visit. You can choose from different deductible options when purchasing your plan, with lower deductibles resulting in slightly higher plan costs. The deductible amount will be clearly stated in your warranty agreement.
