Our Brake Service Process

We follow a comprehensive approach to inspect, maintain, and repair your vehicle's brake system for maximum safety and performance.

1

Multi-Point Brake Inspection

Our technicians perform a thorough visual and mechanical inspection of your entire brake system, including pads, rotors, calipers, lines, and master cylinder to identify any issues or signs of wear.

2

Brake Pad & Rotor Evaluation

We measure brake pad thickness and inspect rotors for scoring, warping, or cracking to determine their current condition and remaining service life, ensuring they meet safety standards.

3

Hydraulic System Analysis

Our experts check your brake fluid for quality, contamination, and moisture content while inspecting all brake lines, hoses, and connections for leaks or deterioration.

4

Caliper & Hardware Inspection

We examine brake calipers, pistons, slides, and all associated hardware for proper function, corrosion, or binding that could affect braking performance and contribute to uneven wear.

5

Service & Performance Verification

After completing necessary repairs or maintenance, we perform a road test to verify proper brake pedal feel, stopping performance, and absence of noise or vibration, ensuring your safety on the road.

Performance Brake Upgrades

Enhance your vehicle's stopping power with our premium performance brake components and upgrades.

High-Performance Brake Pads

Ceramic or metallic compound brake pads that provide superior stopping power and heat resistance for demanding driving conditions.

Drilled & Slotted Rotors

Specially designed rotors that improve heat dissipation, prevent gas buildup, and enhance overall braking performance.

Racing Brake Fluid

High-boiling-point DOT 4 or DOT 5.1 brake fluid that maintains consistent performance under extreme temperatures.

Stainless Steel Brake Lines

Braided stainless steel brake lines that eliminate expansion for improved pedal feel and more precise braking response.

Big Brake Kits

Complete brake system upgrades with larger rotors, multi-piston calipers, and performance pads for maximum stopping power.

Thermal Brake Guards

Heat shields and thermal barriers that protect surrounding components from extreme brake temperatures during performance driving.
